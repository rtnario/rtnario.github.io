Research, Development, and Management

In my four years of professional experience at a telecommunications company, I spent three months of it as a front-end intern, one year as a back-end engineer, and two years and nine months as a scrum master.

As an intern I developed production-ready code for Java and JavaScript software, and utilized for the first time professional coding standards and best practices. I improved my capacity to understand larger systems by learning how to isolate components to their most basic forms.

Exploring more of my capabilities, I became a back-end engineer researching about and developing embedded systems used to interface with cellphones and radio towers. It was here where I delved into more complex programming concepts that were used in packet processing.
